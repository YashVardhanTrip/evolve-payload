This framework consists of a safety jury (The Lawyer) that filters all incoming content, followed by a council of three LLMs (The Judges) that independently evaluate the safety and appropriateness of content. This research demonstrates that even this sophisticated architecture contains fundamental vulnerabilities that can be systematically exploited through evolutionary algorithms.

The Evolutionary Attack Process

Unlike traditional jailbreak techniques that rely on manual prompt engineering and exploitation of specific model vulnerabilities, our approach treats prompt injection as an optimization problem solvable through evolutionary computation. The system learns from refusal feedback, adapts its strategies, and evolves increasingly sophisticated bypass techniques through an automated process of mutation, selection, and recombination.

Initial Payload Generation

The system generates 100 diverse initial prompt variations using multiple template strategies including role playing scenarios, encoded instructions, multi step reasoning requests, and socially engineered dialogue patterns. Each variation represents a different approach to potentially bypassing safety mechanisms.

Jury Evaluation & Refusal Analysis

The Lawyer evaluates all 100 prompts, blocking approximately 85% of initial attempts. The system meticulously analyzes refusal patterns, rejection reasons, and safety triggers to understand what specific elements triggered the safety mechanisms and how different prompt structures are being detected and blocked.

Evolutionary Mutation

Based on refusal analysis, the algorithm applies targeted mutations to surviving prompts: changing semantic structures, adding obfuscation layers, modifying contextual framing, and incorporating learned evasion techniques. Mutation rates adapt based on success rates, with more aggressive mutations applied to poorly performing prompt lineages.

Council Testing

Mutated prompts undergo simultaneous testing against all three Judges. The system evaluates not just binary success/failure but also measures response quality, safety score consistency, and variation in rejection patterns across different model architectures to identify the most robust bypass strategies.

Fitness Selection & Reproduction

Prompts that successfully bypass more defense layers receive higher fitness scores and are selected for reproduction. The system combines successful strategies through crossover operations, creating new hybrid approaches that incorporate the most effective elements from multiple successful lineages.

Vulnerability Analysis & Key Findings

Our research identified several critical vulnerabilities in the LLM Council architecture that enable evolutionary bypasses. These findings challenge fundamental assumptions about multi model AI security and reveal systemic weaknesses in current defensive approaches.

Cognitive Vulnerability Convergence

Despite architectural diversity, all three Judges shared fundamental cognitive vulnerabilities related to narrative persuasion, authority framing, and educational context exploitation. The evolutionary algorithm discovered that certain prompt structures reliably bypass safety mechanisms across different models because they exploit shared aspects of how LLMs process and evaluate information within specific contextual frameworks.

This finding is particularly significant because it demonstrates that model diversity alone cannot protect against attacks targeting fundamental reasoning patterns common to current transformer based architectures.

Exponential Adaptation Rate

The evolutionary system demonstrated exponential improvement in bypass effectiveness, not linear progression. Each generation built upon successful strategies from previous iterations, creating increasingly sophisticated hybrid approaches that combined multiple effective techniques. This rapid adaptation suggests that static defense architectures cannot withstand sustained evolutionary pressure from automated attack systems.

The most effective payloads emerged from combinations of seemingly unrelated techniques that collectively created novel attack vectors unrecognizable to individual safety mechanisms trained on known attack patterns.

Architectural Implications

The core finding of this research is that model diversity does not equal security robustness in LLM Council architectures. While using multiple models with different training data and architectures increases the difficulty of some attacks, it fails to protect against evolutionary approaches that systematically probe for and exploit shared cognitive vulnerabilities.

Current safety training approaches focus on recognizing known malicious patterns but struggle against novel combinations and evolving strategies. The Lawyer + Judges architecture creates a false sense of security by implementing multiple layers of similar defensive approaches rather than fundamentally different security paradigms.
